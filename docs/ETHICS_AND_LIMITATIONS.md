# Ethics and limitations

> **Research and methods demonstration only. Every person, family, event, date and outcome in this repository is entirely synthetic and computer-generated. The repository contains no patient, family, education, hospital, suicide, NAPLAN, Curtin, Australian, United Kingdom or linked administrative data. It is not a clinical, educational or public-sector decision-support system; it must not be used to assess, rank or intervene on any real person or family; and it does not demonstrate data access, clinical validity, causal identification, regulatory compliance or real-world feasibility.**

## Prohibited uses

Do not use this repository to assess, rank, diagnose, triage, intervene on, or make decisions about any real person or family. Do not upload real records. Do not interpret synthetic associations as empirical findings. Do not use demonstration thresholds as intervention thresholds. Do not infer endorsement by Curtin University, Dr Getinet Yaya, any government, health service, school system, education authority, data-linkage unit, or other institution.

## Scientific limitations

The data-generating process is transparent but simplified. Shared family liability is unobserved in normal analyses by design. Sibling fixed effects remove time-invariant shared family factors but not child-specific or pregnancy-specific confounding, carryover, measurement error, selection, or interference. Predictive performance is evaluated only in synthetic held-out families and is not evidence of clinical validity, fairness, transportability, or utility.

## Outcome boundary

A synthetic self-harm-related hospital-presentation variable is shown descriptively at aggregate level. Suicide mortality is not simulated. The primary prediction target is an adverse educational trajectory. No individual risk ranking or person-level prediction export is exposed.
